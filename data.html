Data

There are many organizations working together to create the dataset we used to create our map. They are all bound by a common ideology; Vision Zero. This is a strategy to eliminate all traffic fatalities and severe injuries, while increasing safe, healthy, equitable mobility for all. Their website can be found here:

Vision Zero Network

The data set we used is developed and maintained by the Massachusetts Vision Zero Coalition who advocate for the reduction of traffic injuries and deaths across Massachusetts. The coalition includes community-based organizations, nonprofits, businesses, civic groups and individuals representing communities across the state. Their website can be found here:

Massachusetts Vision Zero Coalition

Pedestrian fatalities are collected based on media reports by WalkBoston. This organization aims to make walking safer and easier in Massachusetts to encourage better health, a cleaner environment and more vibrant communities. Their website can be found here:

Walk Boston

Bicycle fatalities are collected based on media accounts by MassBike. They advocate for policies that encourage and support community wellness, equity, and inclusion, enable sustainable growth, drive economic vitality, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Their website can be found here:

Mass Bike

Finally, the full dataset is hosted by Livable Streets Alliance, who work towards a world where streets are safe, vibrant public spaces that connect people to the places where they live, work, and play. Their website is found here:

Livable Streets Alliance

Currently, the Fatal Data set includes 440 instances with up to 9 attributes covering the years 2016 through 2021. The attributes are:

  • Date of crash
  • Town/City
  • Time of crash
  • Name
  • Age
  • Type (pedestrian or bicyclist)
  • Link to media report
  • Longitude coordinate
  • Latitude coordinate
